# Future Banking Roadmap

## Stage 1: Banking Intelligence Prototype

Status: implemented in Version 4.

- Shared opportunity ranking and next-best-action selection
- Financial health explainability
- Scenario impact heatmaps
- Monthly review history
- Decision journal with expected versus actual outcomes
- Future outcome attribution
- Local product analytics
- Cross-platform executive demo

All data is mock data and all persistence is local to the running product session.

## Stage 2: Governed Customer Data

- Authenticated household workspaces
- Consent and permission records
- Encrypted profile, account, and journal persistence
- Plaid Sandbox behind backend token vaulting
- Data freshness and source metadata
- Export, correction, retention, and deletion controls
- Versioned calculation assumptions

Exit criteria: security, privacy, accessibility, incident-response, vendor, and regulatory reviews are complete.

## Stage 3: Recommendation Governance

- Versioned ranking policies and feature flags
- Recommendation eligibility and suppression rules
- Reason codes and complete calculation lineage
- Human review and policy override workflows
- Fairness, suitability, and adverse-impact testing
- Champion/challenger evaluation
- Drift, calibration, and outcome monitoring

The language model remains outside calculation, eligibility, pricing, and approval decisions.

## Stage 4: Banker-Assisted Intelligence

- Shared customer and banker portfolio review
- Customer-approved scenario collaboration
- Service and product handoffs
- Follow-up plans and outcome tracking
- CRM and core-banking adapters
- White-label design and tenant policy

The next-best action should optimize customer financial outcomes, not only product conversion.

## Stage 5: Continuous Outcome Learning

- Actual-versus-expected ingestion
- Recommendation acceptance and completion funnels
- Causal measurement and experiment governance
- Personalized ranking within approved policies
- Household-level alerts for readiness and goal drift
- Monthly and annual financial readiness reports

## Platform Measures

Track:

- Percentage of users with a clear next action
- Recommendation acceptance and completion
- Modeled and observed customer benefit
- Readiness-score movement
- Goal completion and timeline change
- Explanation comprehension
- Customer trust and opt-out rates
- Policy exceptions and model-risk incidents

Success is not more engagement alone. Success is measurable improvement in customer financial outcomes with understandable, governed decisions.
